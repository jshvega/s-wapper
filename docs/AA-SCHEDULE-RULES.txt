TRAVEL PROFESSIONAL SCHEDULE ADJUSTMENT RULES
(Swaps and Covers)

Each Travel Professional (TP) is assigned a fixed weekly schedule consisting of five (5) scheduled workdays and two (2) assigned days off. Each scheduled workday includes one shift (maximum nine hours including a one-hour lunch). TPs may utilize two adjustment tools to modify their schedules: Swaps and Covers.

––––––––––––––––––––––
SWAPS
––––––––––––––––––––––

A Swap is the exchange of shift times between two TPs on a day when both are scheduled to work.

Swaps are time-based only and are not dependent on role or position. When two TPs complete a Swap, each TP assumes the other’s shift time for that specific day. The underlying workday remains assigned to both TPs; only the scheduled shift time changes.

Swaps may only occur on days when both TPs are originally scheduled to work. A TP may not Swap a shift on a day they are assigned off.

There is no limit to the number of Swaps a TP may participate in. Swaps may be chained, meaning that a TP may Swap a shift and subsequently Swap that newly acquired shift again with another TP on the same or a different day.

Swaps do not affect a TP’s total number of assigned workdays in a week. They only modify the scheduled shift times for those days.

––––––––––––––––––––––
COVERS
––––––––––––––––––––––

A Cover occurs when one TP works a scheduled shift, or a portion of a scheduled shift, on behalf of another TP. Covers may be requested for an entire shift or for a partial shift.

When a Cover is completed, the covering TP assumes responsibility for working the specified time block, and the requesting TP is relieved from working that time block.

Covers may be arranged for any scheduled workday. There are no restrictions based on specific calendar days or holidays.

All Cover agreements are made directly between TPs and do not require managerial approval.

––––––––––––––––––––––
WEEKLY REST REQUIREMENT
––––––––––––––––––––––

Each TP is assigned two (2) designated days off per calendar week. Regardless of any Swaps or Covers, each TP must take at least one (1) of their assigned days off as a full day of rest during that same calendar week.

A TP may not work both of their assigned days off within the same calendar week, even if they are covered by another TP on a different day during that week.

––––––––––––––––––––––
COVER LIMITATIONS
––––––––––––––––––––––

A TP may Cover another TP’s shift no more than once (1) per calendar week.

A TP may request to be Covered for up to five (5) shifts per calendar month. Monthly limits are calculated based on the calendar month in Central Standard Time (CST).

A TP who is Covered on a given day may still Cover another TP on that same day, provided all other applicable rules are followed.

––––––––––––––––––––––
REST BETWEEN SHIFTS
––––––––––––––––––––––

There must be a minimum rest period of eight (8) hours between the end of one worked shift and the beginning of the next worked shift.

This rest requirement applies regardless of whether shifts are worked as part of a TP’s assigned schedule, a Swap, or a Cover arrangement.

––––––––––––––––––––––
GENERAL CONDITIONS
––––––––––––––––––––––

Swaps and Covers may be used together in any order, including performing a Swap prior to a Cover in order to adjust shift times before coverage occurs.

All adjustments made through Swaps or Covers must comply with the rules outlined above.
